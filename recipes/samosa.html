Samosa

Back to Home

Description

Samosa is a fried or baked pastry with a savory filling, such as spiced potatoes, onions, peas, lentils, macaroni, noodles, or minced meat (lamb, beef or chicken). It may take different forms, including triangular, cone, or half-moon shapes, depending on the region.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 cup boiled and mashed potatoes
  • 1/2 cup boiled green peas
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon amchur (dry mango powder)
  • 1/2 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon ginger paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon green chili paste
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  • Combine flour, oil, and salt in a bowl. Add water and knead into a stiff dough. Cover with a damp cloth and set aside for 30 minutes.
  • Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds and fennel seeds. Saute for a minute.
  • Add ginger paste, garlic paste, and green chili paste. Saute for a minute.
  • Add boiled green peas and mashed potatoes. Mix well.
  • Add red chili powder, turmeric powder, coriander powder, garam masala, amchur, chaat masala, and salt. Mix well.
  • Add chopped coriander leaves. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes. Remove from heat and let it cool.
  • Divide the dough into equal portions and roll each portion into a ball. Roll out each ball into a thin oval shape and cut it into two halves.
  • Take one half and fold it into a cone shape. Seal the edges with water.
  • Fill the cone with the prepared stuffing and seal the open edge with water.
  • Heat oil in a pan. Deep fry the samosas until golden brown and crispy.
  • Serve hot with mint chutney or tamarind chutney.
