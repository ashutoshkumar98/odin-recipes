Jalebi

Back to Home

Description

Jalebi is a sweet popular in countries of the Indian Subcontinent such as India, Pakistan, Nepal, and Bangladesh. It is made by deep-frying a wheat flour batter in pretzel or circular shapes, which are then soaked in sugar syrup.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/4 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon saffron strands
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon rose water
  • 1/4 teaspoon lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup ghee

Steps

  • Combine flour, water, baking powder, baking soda, turmeric powder, and saffron strands in a bowl. Mix well to form a smooth batter. Cover and set aside for 12 hours.
  • Combine sugar and water in a pan. Bring to a boil and simmer until it reaches one-string consistency.
  • Add cardamom powder, rose water, and lemon juice to the sugar syrup. Mix well and remove from heat.
  • Heat ghee in a pan. Pour the batter into a squeeze bottle or piping bag.
  • Squeeze the batter into hot ghee in pretzel or circular shapes. Fry until golden brown and crispy.
  • Remove the jalebis from ghee and soak in sugar syrup for 2-3 minutes.
  • Remove the jalebis from the syrup and serve hot or cold.
